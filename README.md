<h2><a class="anchor" id="overview"></a>🔍 Overview</h2>

This project focuses on analyzing hospitality data across multiple cities and properties to understand overall business performance.  
The analysis evaluates revenue trends, booking patterns, occupancy rates, cancellation behavior, and customer ratings to support operational and strategic decision-making.

---

<h2><a class="anchor" id="business-problem"></a>🎯 Business Problem</h2>

The hospitality business required a structured analysis to:

- Monitor revenue performance across cities and properties  
- Track occupancy, ADR, and RevPAR to evaluate operational efficiency  
- Analyze booking behavior across platforms and room classes  
- Identify high cancellation and no-show rates impacting revenue  
- Provide management with data-driven insights for pricing and capacity decisions  

---

<h2><a class="anchor" id="tools--technologies"></a>🛠️ Tools & Technologies</h2>

- Excel — data cleaning, validation, and initial analysis  
- SQL — data extraction, joins, aggregations, and transformation  
- Power BI — data modeling, DAX measures, and interactive dashboards  
- Tableau — trend analysis and comparative visual reporting   
- GitHub — version control and project documentation  

---

<h2><a class="anchor" id="exploratory-data-analysis-eda"></a>📈 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)</h2>

- Analyzed booking distribution across room classes and cities  
- Evaluated monthly and weekly revenue trends  
- Studied booking platform contribution to total bookings  
- Reviewed cancellation and no-show patterns  
- Compared weekday vs weekend revenue performance  

---

<h2><a class="anchor" id="analysis--key-findings"></a>📌 Analysis & Key Findings</h2>

- Total revenue reached **1.71B**, with strong contributions from premium and elite room categories  
- **Mumbai** generated the highest revenue, followed by Bangalore and Hyderabad  
- Occupancy rate averaged **~58%**, indicating moderate capacity utilization  
- Cancellation rate is relatively high at **~24.8%**, impacting realized revenue  
- No-show rate remains low at **~5%**, but still contributes to revenue leakage  
- Majority of bookings come from **third-party platforms**, with “others” and “MakeYourTrip” leading  
- Luxury category contributes a larger share of revenue compared to business category  
- Weekly trends show fluctuations with a noticeable drop in the final week  
- Customer ratings average **3.6**, indicating scope for service improvement
